Cryptocurrency Volume Over Time 2013-2018

Historical Analysis: This visualization tracks the explosive growth of cryptocurrency trading volume during the formative years of 2013-2018. The bar chart shows annual trading volume for major cryptocurrencies, revealing key market phases: the early Bitcoin monopoly (2013), post-Mt. Gox recovery (2014-2015), pre-halving buildup (2016), bull market peak (2017), and market correction (2018).

How to read: Each bar shows annual trading volume. Higher bars indicate more trading activity. The dramatic growth from 2016 to 2017 shows the bull market impact.

2013-2018 Volume Insights

Volume Growth Patterns

The visualization reveals exponential volume growth starting in 2016, peaking in 2017 with $70B daily volume. Bitcoin dominated early years (2013-2015) but altcoins gained significant volume share during the 2017 bull market.

Market Structure Evolution

Early years (2013-2014) showed single-exchange dominance (Mt. Gox). By 2017, multiple global exchanges distributed volume. The 2018 correction filtered out low-volume assets.

Volume Concentration Trends

Bitcoin volume concentration decreased from 90%+ (2013) to 38% (2017) before recovering to 50%+ (2018). Ethereum grew from 0% (2014) to 18% (2017).

Volatility & Volume Correlation

High volume periods (2017) correlated with extreme price volatility. Low volume periods (2014, 2018) showed price consolidation. The 2016 halving created sustained volume increase before price surge.

Historical Significance

The 2013-2018 period represents cryptocurrency's first complete market cycle. Volume patterns established during these years created foundational liquidity structures that enabled later institutional participation and market maturation.
