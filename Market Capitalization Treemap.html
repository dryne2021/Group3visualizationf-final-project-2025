Market Capitalization Treemap 2013-2018

Historical Analysis: This treemap visualization tracks the dramatic transformation of cryptocurrency market structure from Bitcoin's early monopoly (2013: 95% dominance) to the diversified ecosystem of 2018. The visualization shows how market capitalization was distributed among different cryptocurrencies during each year of this formative period, revealing the emergence of Ethereum, the 2017 ICO boom, and the market correction of 2018.

Historical Insight: The treemap clearly shows Bitcoin's shrinking dominance from a massive rectangle in 2013 to just 38% of the visualization in 2017. The 2018 view shows many of the 2017 ICO tokens have disappeared or significantly shrunk.

2013-2018 Market Structure Evolution

Early Market Structure (2013-2015)

  • 2013: Bitcoin 95% dominance - near monopoly
  • 2014: Post-Mt. Gox market cap drops 50%
  • 2015: Ethereum launch begins diversification
  • Early altcoins: Litecoin, Ripple, Monero emerge

Bull Market Expansion (2016-2017)

  • 2016: Bitcoin halving anticipation builds
  • 2017: ICO boom creates hundreds of new tokens
  • Bitcoin dominance drops to 38% (Dec 2017)
  • Ethereum reaches 18% market share

Market Correction (2018)

  • Market cap drops 75% from $820B to $200B
  • Many 2017 ICO tokens disappear
  • Bitcoin dominance recovers to 52%
  • Surviving assets show more stable structure

Historical Significance 2013-2018

The 2013-2018 period represents cryptocurrency's complete first market cycle. The treemap visualization shows this evolution from Bitcoin's early monopoly through explosive diversification and back to consolidation. This period established the basic market structure patterns that would repeat in future cycles, with Bitcoin dominance acting as a key market cycle indicator.
