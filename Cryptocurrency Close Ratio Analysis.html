Close Ratio Formula

Close Ratio measures daily performance by comparing closing price to opening price. In early cryptocurrency markets, ratios showed extreme volatility with values ranging from 0.5 (50% daily decline) to 1.8 (80% daily gain).

Close Ratio = Closing Price ÷ Opening Price

Ratio > 1: Positive day (green), Ratio < 1: Negative day (red)

Cryptocurrency Close Ratio Analysis 2013-2018

Historical Context: This heatmap visualization shows the extreme volatility of early cryptocurrency markets (2013-2018). Each cell represents a cryptocurrency's close ratio for a specific year, with color intensity showing performance strength. The visualization reveals the dramatic 2014 bear market, the recovery of 2015-2016, the explosive 2017 bull market, and the 2018 correction.

Historical Insight: The heatmap clearly shows the 2014 bear market (mostly red), the 2017 bull market (mostly dark green), and how different cryptocurrencies performed during these extreme market conditions.

2013-2018 Performance Patterns

Early Market Volatility (2013-2015)

  • 2013: Extreme volatility, ratios 0.7-1.5
  • 2014: Bear market, most ratios below 0.8
  • 2015: Recovery begins, ratios near 1.0
  • Early altcoins show higher volatility than Bitcoin

Bull Market Performance (2016-2017)

  • 2016: Pre-halving buildup, ratios 1.1-1.3
  • 2017: Extreme ratios 1.3-1.8 common
  • Altcoins outperform Bitcoin significantly
  • ICO tokens show highest ratios (1.5+)

Market Correction (2018)

  • Ratios drop to 0.7-0.9 range
  • Highest volatility assets correct most
  • Bitcoin shows relative stability
  • Many 2017 ICOs show ratios below 0.5
